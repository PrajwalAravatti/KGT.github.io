Course Learning Reflections

Reflections on Problem-Solving Techniques, Efficiency, Design Principles, and More

1) What are the kinds of problems we see in nature?

The fundamental problem-solving techniques are seen in natural processes:

  • Iteration: Can be seen in natural cycles like day and night and organisms adapting to environmental conditions.
  • Recursion: Found in the branching of trees and the structure of the DNA molecule.
  • Backtracking: Visible in the process of natural selection and Darwin's theory, where organisms prune non-functional traits.

2) Space and Time Efficiency

Space Efficiency: Refers to how much extra space an algorithm requires apart from the input.

Time Efficiency: Measures the total running time of an algorithm.

Efficient algorithms are crucial for building high-performance, scalable, and cost-effective software systems.

3) Takeaways from Design Principles

  • Decomposition: Break complex problems into smaller sub-problems.
  • Pattern Recognition: Identify recurring structures for known solutions.
  • Abstraction: Focus on essential details to simplify complexity.
  • Lazy Propagation: Delay computations until absolutely necessary.
  • Sliding Window: Use a dynamic subset of data to maintain efficiency.

4) Hierarchical Data

  • Tree: A non-linear data structure.
  • Binary Search Tree (BST): Allows efficient search, insertion, and deletion.
  • AVL Tree: A self-balancing BST for efficient operations.
  • Red-Black Tree: A self-balancing BST using color-coding.
  • Heap: A tree-based data structure for implementing priority queues.

5) Array Query Algorithms

Array query algorithms are essential for efficiently retrieving information from a dataset, especially for frequent range queries.

Applications:

  • Websites like Amazon filter products based on price ranges.
  • Google Maps helps find nearby places based on the user's location.
  • Financial platforms analyze stock performance over a specific period.

7) Sorting and Searching Algorithms

  • Sorting: Bubble Sort, Selection Sort, Insertion Sort, Quicksort, Mergesort, Heap Sort.
